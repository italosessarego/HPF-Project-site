        What you can measure

        Transfer · Training
        04 / 06

        Transfer the knowledge — the same way, every time

        Once the method is captured, remove the trainer-to-trainer variation so every associate learns it the same, complete way — and you can see exactly who is ready for the floor.

        • ✓Step title = the exact skill the new associate needs to learn
        • ✓Instructions + media show the trainer how to teach it and what good looks like
        • ✓Assign to one trainee or bulk-assign across a crew
        • ✓A simple checkmark per skill — not a pass/fail grade
        • ✓The training matrix updates in real time as items are checked

        Trainer instructions

        Walk the RF Gladiator login and the Recv Item Lvl path; verify pack qty before entering quantity.

        Bottom-right corner, facing out, one per pallet per SKU — so putaway can read it.

        Each step carries the skill to learn, the trainer’s instructions on what not to forget, and photos and videos of the proper method — so the same skill is taught the same way no matter who delivers it.

        Why it matters — showing trainers exactly how to teach each skill, with the proper method on screen, is what removes variation from trainer to trainer.

        What you can measure

        Continuous Coaching · Observations
        05 / 06

        Coach the standard so it holds

        Training gets them ready. Observation keeps them there — quantifying whether people still follow the method, and coaching the gaps in real time before corners get cut.

        • ✓Targets — set how many observations each observer owes per day or week
        • ✓Context first — prior trend and most-missed prompts before they start
        • ✓Summary & coach — immediate feedback on what to improve, then schedule a follow-up
        • ✓Auto-assigned — the system picks who to observe, so it’s never just the convenient person
        • ✓Guided prompts — what to look for, with notes and photo evidence as they go

        What makes an observation actually work

        01 Specific

        Job-specific, not generic — the observer checks this procedure’s steps, not a one-size form.

        02 Enough sample

        Observations actually get done, spread across people — a big enough sample that the numbers mean something, with no pencil-whipping.

        03 Real coaching

        A coaching session right after to fix mistakes in real time, then a follow-up to confirm the fix stuck.

        Observation runner · prompt 3 of 6

        Each prompt carries the key steps to watch for, the standard the associate should be hitting, and photos and videos of the proper method — so every observer knows what to look for, no matter who’s observing.

        Global questions — surface systemic issues. Beyond the prompts, each observation can ask: What’s preventing them from doing their job? Any improvement ideas? — turning the floor into a source of fixes that feed back to Capture.
