Methodology

Best practices
for process compliance.

How we turn scattered know-how into one standard — captured once, transferred the same way every time, and coached so it holds on the floor. The playbook below is drawn from solving this across multiple operations.

At a glance

Produces SOPs — the single source of truth

Transfer

Coaching

What it replaces

The same three gaps, every time

Before a system like this exists, the cracks show up in the same three places. Each stage of the loop closes one of them.

Procedures

No single source of truth

  • ×SOPs scattered across formats and SharePoint
  • ×Collaboration lost across Teams, email and meetings
  • ×Documents drift out of date with no correctness check
  • ×No proof anyone read an update
Transfer

No standard way to train

  • ×Training depends entirely on who’s teaching
  • ×Different trainers teach the same thing differently
  • ×Records live on paper and spreadsheets
  • ×No cross-site visibility, so weak sites stay hidden
Continuous Coaching

No way to know it’s followed

  • ×No systematic way to quantify adherence
  • ×Generic forms not tied to the SOP
  • ×Pencil-whipped records; only the easy person observed
  • ×Notes on problems, but no coaching and no trends
Capture · SOPs

What is the right way to do the work?

Make the proper method the single source of truth — current, governed, and impossible to lose track of. Everything downstream derives from here.

Transfer · Training

Is everyone trained the same way?

Each step carries the skill to learn, the trainer’s instructions on what not to forget, and photos and videos of the proper method — so the same skill is taught the same way no matter who delivers it.

Key capabilities

  • ✓Each step mapped to the exact skill to learn
  • ✓Trainer instructions and media for what good looks like
  • ✓Assignment to one trainee or a whole crew
  • ✓A simple checkmark per skill, not a pass/fail grade
  • ✓A training matrix that updates in real time

Continuous Coaching · Observations

Do people still follow procedures after training?

Each prompt carries the key steps to watch for, the standard the associate should be hitting, and photos and videos of the proper method — so every observer knows what to look for, no matter who’s observing.

Key capabilities

  • ✓Observation targets set per observer, by day or week
  • ✓Prior trend and most-missed prompts shown up front
  • ✓Immediate coaching feedback, then a scheduled follow-up
  • ✓Auto-assigned subjects, never just the convenient person
  • ✓Guided prompts with notes and photo evidence
