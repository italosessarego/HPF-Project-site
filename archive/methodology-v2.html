How we turn scattered know-how into one standard — captured once, transferred the same way every time, and coached so it holds on the floor. The playbook below is drawn from solving this across multiple operations.

What it replaces

The same three gaps, every time

Before a system like this exists, the cracks show up in the same three places. Each stage of the loop closes one of them.

The model

Capture, transfer, coach

Three stages, each answering one question and producing one artifact. The artifact carries forward — a captured SOP becomes the training, the training becomes the thing you observe against — and what the floor surfaces returns to the start.

↻

The loop closes. Improvement ideas and gaps surfaced during coaching feed back into Capture — so the standard gets sharper every cycle instead of drifting out of date.
