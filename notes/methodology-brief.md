# Methodology — creative brief (new page)

> **This is a brief for a brand-new page, designed from scratch.** Assume there is no existing methodology page — nothing to reference, preserve, port, or clean up. You have the content and data below and the meetBean design system. Design the page that lands the argument best. Decide the structure, the sections, and the components yourself; nothing here dictates layout.

---

## What this page is

A page that sells meetBean's **methodology for process compliance** to the Harbor Freight operations leadership — the people who own consistency across sites. It argues for an *approach*, then shows that the platform runs it. It is persuasion, not documentation.

**Audience:** ops leaders and site/functional managers. They feel the pain of: SOPs nobody can find or trust, training that depends on who's teaching, and no real way to know the standard is actually followed. They care about consistency across sites, proof/auditability, less rework, and reducing key-person risk.

**Voice:** confident, plain, operations-literate. Short sentences. No hype, no jargon. Calm authority — "we've solved this before."

---

## The one thing this page must do

Convince them that scattered, person-dependent know-how can become **one enforced standard** through a continuous loop:

**Capture → Transfer → Continuous Coaching**  (i.e. SOPs → Training → Observations)

…where each stage produces an artifact the next one builds on, adherence becomes a number you can manage, and the floor feeds improvements back to the start. It's a *system*, not a document and not a tool.

## What the reader should walk away believing

1. **There's a real method here, not just software** — a clear three-part loop they can picture.
2. **Each part is concrete and measurable** — real capabilities, and metrics that tell them whether it's working.
3. **It fits how they already run** — configured to their sites and job functions; everyone sees only their part.
4. **It compounds** — one captured procedure becomes training and observation; adherence becomes managed; gaps feed back into better procedures.

## The substance (raw material — compose it; don't just transcribe it)

This is the content the page needs to convey, organized by idea. Copy is usable as-is or as a starting point. **You decide what becomes a hero moment, what's a supporting detail, and what gets cut for focus.**

### The method (the spine of the argument)
A loop of three stages; each answers a question and produces an artifact:

| Stage | The question it answers | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| **Capture** | What is the right way to do the work? | **SOPs** — a single, governed source of truth |
| **Transfer** | Is everyone trained the same way? | **Trainings** — taught identically, with a record of who's mastered what |
| **Continuous Coaching** | Do people still follow it after training? | **Observations** — adherence quantified and coached in real time |

The loop closes: improvement ideas and gaps surfaced during coaching feed back into Capture. "Each step produces an artifact the next one builds on."

### The problem it solves (use as much or as little as lands)
The same three gaps recur before a system like this exists:
- **Around SOPs:** no single source of truth — they live in scattered formats/SharePoint; collaboration is lost across Teams/email/meetings; documents drift out of date with no correctness check; no proof anyone read an update.
- **Around training:** no standard way to train — it depends on the trainer; different trainers teach the same thing differently; records live on paper/spreadsheets; no cross-site visibility, so weak sites stay hidden.
- **Around observation:** no systematic way to know people follow the process or to quantify adherence; generic forms not tied to the SOP; pencil-whipped records / always observing the easy person; notes on problems but no coaching and no trends across sites.

### What each stage actually does + what it lets you measure
*(Capabilities and the kinds of questions the reporting answers. Group, trim, or reframe as the design wants.)*

**Capture — the SOP source of truth**
- Does: one canvas showing status / version / author / last-updated; photos & video embedded in the procedure; full version history with red-lined diffs; acknowledgment tracked automatically; draft → review → approve → acknowledge governance built in.
- Measure: SOP compliance (everything reviewed yearly? every job function covered? anything expiring?); review workload (what's due, prioritized by expiry); supervisor compliance (are reviewers/acknowledgers keeping up? repeat offenders?).

**Transfer — training without trainer drift**
- Does: each step is the exact skill to learn, with trainer instructions and "what good looks like" in media; assign to one trainee or a whole crew; a simple checkmark per skill (not pass/fail); a training matrix that updates live.
- Measure: training coverage (who's trained on what; gaps by function/site); upcoming/overdue training; trainer compliance (trained on time? time-to-train by site; sites that lag).

**Continuous Coaching — adherence you can manage**
- Does: targets per observer; auto-assigned subjects (not just the convenient person); guided prompts of what to look for with notes/photo evidence; a graded scale (not pass/fail) that auto-scores to an adherence %; immediate coaching + scheduled follow-up; "global questions" that surface systemic blockers and improvement ideas (the feedback into Capture).
- Measure: are observations being done (observers vs. targets; worst functions/sites); process adherence (score by site/function/prompt; most-failed prompts; where practice drifts); correlations (observer vs. scores; trainers vs. downstream scores).

### It fits their operation
- meetBean configures the platform to **their sites and job functions**, mapped on day one.
- Every screen is **scoped to the person's role** — an associate sees their trainings and today's to-dos; a supervisor sees their people plus trainings/observations; a site manager sees their site's functions and dashboards; an executive sees dashboards across every site.
- Each person opens to **exactly what they need to do today** — less time deciding what to do.

### The payoff (the closing note)
"From one procedure to **a living compliance program**." One captured SOP becomes the basis for training, observation, and certification — a unified system, not a pile of documents.

## Design direction

- **Build entirely from the meetBean design system.** Calm, monochrome (off-white / near-black / grays) with **sage as the single accent**, used sparingly. Full-bleed, flat surfaces (no shadows), hairline rules, one typeface (DM Sans) + mono micro-labels, generous space.
- **Match the feel of the team's favorite pages — Project Overview and Implementation Plan.** That means: an "At a glance" band after the hero, grouped capability **cards / boards** (a bordered card with an intro on one side and grouped ✓-checklists on the other is a strong, on-brand pattern), equal grids of peer cards, restraint over density.
- **Fewer component types = calmer.** Reuse the sanctioned set (cards, the metric/at-a-glance tile, pills, tags, the breakdown diagram, the closing statement band). Don't invent new component shapes; if something needs a custom treatment, question whether it's worth it.
- **Lead with the loop.** The three-stage method is the spine — make sure a reader gets it fast and remembers it. Everything else supports it.
- **No prescribed structure.** Choose the narrative and section set that makes the argument land. Let the strongest version win, even if it looks nothing like a conventional features page.
